Overview

The dataset used in this analysis originates from UNICEF and covers the period from 1970 to 2022. It includes data on the under-five mortality rate, which is measured as the number of deaths of children under five years of age per 1,000 live births across various countries. The dataset contains 11,651 entries with three columns: Geographic Area, TIME_PERIOD, and OBS_VALUE. The under-five mortality rate is a critical indicator of child health and overall development in a country. It reflects the number of children who die before reaching the age of five, per 1,000 live births, within a given time frame. This measure is crucial for assessing and monitoring the progress of child health interventions and programs aimed at reducing child mortality rates worldwide.
